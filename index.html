David Harborth

I am a doctoral candidate at the Chair of Mobile Business and Multilateral Security at Goethe University Frankfurt, Frankfurt am Main, Germany. My supervisor is Professor Kai Rannenberg. From January to March 2020 I joined the Usable Security and Privacy Group at the International Computer Science Institute (ICSI) and the Berkeley Laboratory for Usable and Experimental Security (BLUES) as a visiting researcher. Prior to joining Goethe University as a doctoral candidate I was working as a Senior Associate at KPMG AG in the Financial Services area. Click here to download my complete CV.

Research Interests

My research covers three main areas:
  • User interactions and perceptions regarding Augmented Reality (AR), especially related to privacy
  • User-centric analyses of privacy-enhancing technologies
  • Technology acceptance with a special focus on new concepts from related disciplines
