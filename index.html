Dr. David Harborth

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Chair of Mobile Business and Multilateral Security at Goethe University Frankfurt, Frankfurt am Main, Germany. I received my PhD in information systems (business informatics) in 2021 with the grade summa cum laude. My supervisor was Professor Kai Rannenberg. From January to March 2020 I joined the Usable Security and Privacy Group at the International Computer Science Institute (ICSI) and the Berkeley Laboratory for Usable and Experimental Security (BLUES) as a visiting researcher. Prior to joining Goethe University as a doctoral candidate I was working as a Senior Associate at KPMG AG in the Financial Services area. Click here to download my complete CV.

Research Interests

My research covers three main areas:
  • User perceptions related to immersive systems and technology acceptance research, especially Augmented Reality (AR)
  • Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Intelligence Augmentation (IA) and possible effects on the work life
  • Privacy of individuals in the digital sphere, including analyses of privacy concerns and their ramifications with regard to technology use
  • Informational self-determination of individuals through privacy-enhancing technologies (PETs) and user adoption
  • Technology acceptance with a special focus on new concepts from related disciplines
